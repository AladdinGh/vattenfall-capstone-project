# Day 1 Orientation

Day 1 is the point where the capstone begins to feel real.

You are not being asked to create a random teaching repository.  
You are being asked to create the foundation of a multi-day lakehouse project that should look credible in your own GitHub account.

That means you should approach Day 1 with three ideas in mind.

First, you are making design decisions.  
Every folder, file, and document should exist for a reason. A strong repository makes those reasons visible.

Second, you are building for the rest of the week.  
Day 1 should prepare Day 2 bronze work, Day 3 silver work, and Day 5 gold, governance, testing, and reporting work. If your Day 1 design is weak, the rest of the week becomes harder.

Third, you are building something you could later mention in your portfolio.  
That means your repository should already look thoughtful, structured, and professional.
